Okay, let's break this down step-by-step:
* There are some books that need to be packaged together, with the same number of books per package
* 7/12 of the books were packaged into 14 packages, with 35 books left over
* The remaining books, plus the 35 left over from the first packaging, were packaged into 11 more packages
* So:
    - 7/12 of the books were packaged into 14 packages
    - That means the total number of books originally was (7/12) * 14 = 98
    - 35 books were left over from that first packaging
    - So there were originally 98 + 35 = 133 books
    - The remaining books plus the 35 leftover were packaged into 11 more packages
    - So the number of remaining books must be a multiple of 11 (since there were 11 equal packages)
    - The number of remaining books is 133 - 98 = 35
    - Since 35 is a multiple of 11, the total number of books must have been 133
Therefore, the total number of books originally was 133
